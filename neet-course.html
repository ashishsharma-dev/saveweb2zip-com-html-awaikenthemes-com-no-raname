Frequently asked questions

NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is a national-level medical entrance exam in India for students aspiring to pursue MBBS, BDS, AYUSH, and other medical courses in top medical colleges. It is the only medical entrance exam for admissions in government and private institutions.

Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), NEET UG is the single gateway for admissions to medical programs across the country, replacing multiple state-level and institutional exams to streamline the process.

The exam typically takes place annually, with the 2025 edition expected to follow a similar timeline to previous years, likely in May or June, though exact dates would depend on NTA's official announcement.

To be eligible for NEET, a candidate must:

  • Be at least 17 years old at the time of admission.
  • Have completed or be appearing in Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) as mandatory subjects.
  • Have secured a minimum of 50% marks in PCB for General category (40% for SC/ST/OBC).

We offer multiple course durations:

  • 1-Year NEET Preparation Program – Ideal for Class 12 students.
  • 2-Year NEET Foundation Course – Designed for Class 11 students.
  • Crash Course for NEET – A short-term intensive program for last-minute preparation.

We provide both online and offline classes to cater to students' preferences. Live online sessions with expert faculty and recorded lectures are available for flexible learning.

A minimum of 6-8 hours of focused study per day is recommended, including regular revision and mock tests. Our structured timetable helps students manage their time efficiently.

It assesses candidates in three core subjects:

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Biology (Botany and Zoology)

Exam Structure:

  • Total of 200 multiple-choice questions (180 to be attempted)
  • Maximum Marks: 720
  • Duration: 3 hours and 20 minutes

  • Mode: Pen and Paper
  • Languages: English, Hindi, and several regional languages
